I've reviewed the game's current implementation and believe it has significant
  potential for increased creativity, particularly in how it leverages its core
  themes of "malleability of truth" and "redefining reality."


  The current implementation provides a solid foundation, but the interactions
  with keywords and the environment could be more dynamic and integrated with the
  narrative. To enhance creativity, I propose the following revisions, which may
  deviate from the original design plan as necessary:


   1. Dynamic Player State via Keywords:
       * Instead of simply unlocking movement with the "STATIC" keyword, the
         player will start with a "heavy" state (slower movement, higher
         gravity).
       * Removing the "STATIC" keyword from the environment will revert the
         player's state to normal, simulating the removal of a narrative
         constraint.
       * Introduce a separate "HEAVY" keyword that, if applied to the player or
         another object, dynamically alters its physics (e.g., increasing gravity
         or mass).


   2. Enhanced "Sentence Rule" Implementation:
       * Fully implement the design document's "Sentence Rule": if a keyword is
         dropped in empty space, it should visibly fade and animate its return to
         its original position within its source sentence over a set duration
         (e.g., 3 seconds). This adds a crucial "semantic" layer to the
         drag-and-drop mechanic.


   3. Advanced "Pen" and "Lava" Interaction with "WATER" Counter:
       * The "Pen" will write "The floor is lava." as designed.
       * This will activate a specific "lava" state on the main floor redaction
         bar.
       * Introduce a new "WATER" keyword. The player must find and drag this
         "WATER" keyword onto the "lava" floor to neutralize the hazardous
         effect, directly counteracting the Pen's narrative.


  These changes aim to make the game's mechanics more deeply tied to its thematic
  elements, offering a richer and more genuinely creative player experience.


  I will begin by refining the "STATIC" keyword interaction and the player's
  initial state. This involves adjusting the player's initial physics properties
  to represent being "heavy" or "static," and then implementing the logic to
  normalize these properties once the "STATIC" keyword is effectively "removed"
  from the environment.
